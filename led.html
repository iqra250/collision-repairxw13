FAQS

What does LED stand for?

LED stands for Light Emitting Diode, which is a semiconductor device that emits light when an electric current passes through it.

How does LED technology work?

LED technology works by allowing electrons to recombine with holes in a semiconductor material, which releases energy in the form of photons (light).

What are the advantages of using LED lighting?

LED lighting offers several advantages, including energy efficiency, longer lifespan, lower heat emission, and a wide range of color options.

Can LED lights be used outdoors?

Yes, many LED lights are designed for outdoor use and are often weather-resistant, making them suitable for lighting pathways, gardens, and outdoor spaces.

How do I choose the right color temperature for my space?

The right color temperature depends on the desired ambiance: warm white (around 2700K) is cozy for living spaces, cool white (around 4000K) works well in work areas, and daylight (around 5000K-6500K) is ideal for task lighting.
